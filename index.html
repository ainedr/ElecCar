Popular models

Tesla Roadster (2008-2012)

The company's first commercial all-electric vehicle. The first production vehicle launched into deep space on a Falcon Heavy rocket in a test flight in 2018

Tesla Model S (2012-still)

Third fastest production car ever made. The world's first electric car, awarded in 2013 the most honorable award "MotorTrend Car of the Year" and the highest positive ratings among owners.

Tesla Model X (2014-still)

The fastest accelerating crossover ever made (Model X P100D modification). In 2017, he was given a provocative assessment: "Makes every ICE car on the highway seem like a clumsy and unwieldy relic of the 20th century."

Tesla Model 3 (2017-still)

This is the cheapest Tesla model. The minimum price was $35,000. The manufacturer can hardly cope with the overwhelming demand. In the first 24 hours, more than 180,000 electric vehicles were ordered for $7.5 billion.

Tesla Model Y (2020-still)

Designed as an EV between the fuel-efficient Model 3 sedan (70% of total parts) and the Model X luxury crossover. Offers plenty of space and is better winterized than any other model.

Tesla Cybertruck (2022)

The concept of a powerful Cybertruck electric pickup with bold angular body shapes and armored glass was first shown in 2019. The implementation of the project is constantly postponed. The last time production was postponed to 2022.

Tesla Semi (2023)

The 2016 concept of an all-electric truck tractor. Tesla has two electric truck prototypes. One of them is being tested by the company for internal corporate tasks.

Tesla Cyberquad (2023)

All-electric off-road (ATV) ATV. Conceptually and commercially ready for production. Not released before the launch of the Cybertruck.
